What I Do, What I Use, and How I Do It

We live in a mobile World that's full of exciting new products and services, and with that the adoption rate of mobile devices has quickly become the fastest growing consumer-driven technology of all-time. With the ever changing mobile landscape it's critical for your website to be fluid, and have the ability to adapt to whatever device the viewer's using.

This process is called Responsive Web Design or RWD and, coincidently, this is where I come in. Instead of me talk about, what may sound like a foreign language, common RWD practices such as break points, frameworks, and media queries I'm just going to show you.

Below are two screenshots of everyone's favorite, newegg. The image on your left is your basic, static, web design. That doesn't look too easy to navigate from a phone if you were to ask me. The screenshot to the left is newegg's mobile website. These are actually two different website's, which is frowned upon by Google, but regardless you're provided a better experience if you're having to use your phone.

What I strive to do is build websites that provide your customers' the best possible user experience when they're viewing your website ‐ whether they're using a 3.5" smartphone or a 30" display. I'm not just building you a website, but also providing you a value-added service to help attract and retain new customers by giving them a enjoyable experience while viewing your website.
